I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Agricultural and Resource Economics at Colorado State University and will be on the job market during the 2026–2027 academic year.

I am an applied microeconomist studying key environmental challenges in energy and water use. My research examines how economic incentives and policy design shape consumer and firm decisions, with a focus on agricultural resource management, electric vehicle, and electricity markets. Using empirical industrial organization and causal inference methods, I provide evidence that informs environmental, energy, and agricultural policy. I received my M.S. from the University of Tennessee, Knoxville.
